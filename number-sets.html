The Field Axioms of Arithmetic

Natural numbers
Are the counting numbers:
\(1\), \(2\), \(3\), \(4\), \(5\), \(\ldots\)

Integers
Are the whole numbers,
Positive, negative, and zero,
With no fractional or decimal part:
\(0\), \(1\), \(-1\), \(2\), \(-2\), \(\ldots\)

Rational numbers
Are the numbers
We can express as fractions
Of integers.
Written with a fraction bar and
Integers on top and
Integers on bottom,
Reduced or unreduced.
We may also find them hiding
In plain sight as
Terminating or repeating decimals.
Natural numbers and integers
Are disguised within.

Real numbers
Are the numbers
We find on the number line.
They are all the lengths and distances
In the world around us.
The number line seems full
With just the terminating decimals
And fuller still with the repeating decimals
But the rationals are as nothing
Compared to all the decimals that are.

Irrational as it may seem,
The irrational numbers,
The numbers whose decimals
Neither terminate nor repeat,
Complement the rational numbers to
Complete the real numbers.

Closure
A set of numbers
With an operation \(*\)
Is closed
When applying the operation
To two of the numbers
Results in the same type of number.
